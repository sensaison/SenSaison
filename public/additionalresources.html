What is citizen science?

Citizen science is the involvement of the general public in the scientific process. This process includes, but is not limited to, collecting data, conducting experiments, analyzing data, interpretating results, and writing. Most commonly, citizen science projects are conducted in collaboration with professional scientists, and involve the general public at the data collection or experimental stage. Scientists benefit by obtaining data on a scale that is not possible to them alone and by being exposed to new ideas and perspectives. Members of the public benefit through increased understanding of the natural world and the scientific process, and how those relate to their own daily lives.
